# Project Notes

A few notes so this repo has some context behind it.

I built this as a small portfolio lab while practicing OSINT and cyber threat intelligence basics. I did not want it to be only a list of commands, because in real analysis the important part is usually the reasoning after the command output.

The cases are fictional. I used made-up domains because I did not want to point a public portfolio project at real companies or real suspicious infrastructure. That also means some outputs are simulated. I tried to keep them realistic enough to explain the workflow without pretending they came from a live investigation.

## What I think is good enough for now

- the cases have a clear scope
- the evidence is separated from final reporting
- the reports include limitations
- the Python tool stays passive and simple
- the project shows how I think through weak indicators

## What is still rough

The risk scoring is basic. It is useful for this lab, but I would not use it in a real environment without improving the logic and testing it against more examples.

The cases also do not include screenshots or exported tool output files. I may add those later, but I wanted the first version to stay readable.

## Why I left some uncertainty in the reports

I left phrases like "not conclusive" and "would require further validation" on purpose. In OSINT work I think it is better to be clear about uncertainty than to make the report sound stronger than the evidence.
